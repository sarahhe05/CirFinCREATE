Carbon Footprint

A carbon footprint measures the greenhouse gas emissions (primarily carbon dioxide) that are produced by human activities. These emissions contribute to climate change by trapping heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to rising global temperatures and disruptions to ecosystems. The carbon footprint impacts our daily lives by affecting food security, health, and the environment. Reducing our carbon footprint is essential to ensure a sustainable future for generations to come. By making conscious choices in energy use and consumption, we can collectively lower our impact on the planet.
